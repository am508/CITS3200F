Mental Health

Research shows that having diabetes more than doubles the risk of depression. Depression can increase the likelihood of developing other diabetes complications. Depression is a serious illness that can make it hard to do normal activities and function day to day.

Depression has a lot of signs and symptoms to watch out for. A person may be depressed if for more than two weeks they have:

  • Felt sad, down or miserable most of the time or
  • Lost interest or pleasure in most of their usual activities and
  • Experienced symptoms in a least three of the following groups:
    • Behaviour
      • Stopped going out
      • Unable to concentrate
      • Withdrawn from friends and family
      • Stopped doing things you like
    • Thoughts
      • "I'm worthless"
      • "Nothing good ever happens to me"
      • "I'm a failure"
    • Feelings
      • Sad
      • Irritable
      • Guilty
      • Overwhelmed
    • Physical
      • Trouble sleeping
      • Always tired
      • Headaches and muscle pain

The treatment of depression and diabetes involves monitoring diabetes control and depression symptoms. It is important to talk to your doctor to find a treatment that works best for you. Reaching out to family and friends for support is also crucial.

Nervous System

Neuropathy is a long term complication which affects the nerves. Nerves carry messages between the brain and the various parts of the body. Nerves also carry signals which handle a lot of bodily functions that people aren't aware of. Neuropathy is when high blood glucose levels damage the small blood vessels which go to the nerves. This keeps essential nutrients from reaching the nerves which damages them.

Neuropathy can affect the nerves that carry information to organs and glands which control fuctions without you thinking about them. This can cause a loss of bladder control, sweating and other issues. Neuropathy can also affect the nerves which control movement. This can lead to weakness and loss of muscle in the affected areas.

The best way to avoid neuropathy is to keep your blood glucose levels in your target range and to have regular check-ups.
