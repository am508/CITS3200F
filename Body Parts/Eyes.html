The retina is the part of the eye that sees. It is a layer of cells in the back of the eye. When light enters the eye, the retina changes any light that reaches it into signals that are sent to the brain, which turns the signals into the images you see. Damage to the blood vessels of the retina can affect vision.

It is very important to look after your eyes. You can do this by getting regular check-ups at least every two years. The early stages may not have any symptoms so getting checked can help keep your eyes healthy. Maintaining healthy blood pressure and cholesterol levels are also important to protect your eyes.

Symptoms of eye problems can include:

  • Blurred, patchy vision that can't be corrected with glasses
  • Problems with balance, reading, watching TV or recognizing people
  • Over-sensitivity to glare
  • Difficulty seeing at night
If you notice any of these symptoms, make an appointment to see your doctor.
